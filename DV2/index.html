Migration into Australia

Migration has always been a big identity of Australia ever since the first European settlers, the First Fleet in 1788 captained by Arthur Philip and are also made of convicts, soldiers and their families. Now, the migration pattern has changed and migrants are coming from all over the world. Below is a choropleth map depicting Australia’s overseas born population. The key regions are the United Kingdom, New Zealand, the US and Canada being the main English speaking countries. In Asia, countries like China, India and the Philippines and in Southeast Asia like Vietnam, Indonesia and Malaysia. Overall, the migration distribution has a predominant Asia Pacific centred migration pattern signifying Australia’s strong diversity and cultural differences.

Australia’s migration profile highlights a skilled, diverse, and globally connected population. Most new arrivals in 2023–24 entered through the Skill Stream, while the Family Stream came as the second most popular migration stream. The leading migrant occupations—such as registered nurses, accountants, software developers, and engineers—show strong demand in healthcare, technology, and infrastructure sectors. Linguistically, Australia remains highly multicultural, with Mandarin, Arabic, Vietnamese, Cantonese, Punjabi, Italian, and Greek among the top languages spoken at home other than English. Together, these trends demonstrate Australia’s reliance on skilled migration, its expanding cultural diversity, and its deepening ties to the Asia-Pacific region.
