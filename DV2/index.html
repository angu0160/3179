Migration to Australia

An insight into Australia's migration pattern

Overview

Migration has always been a big identity of Australia ever since the first European settlers, the First Fleet in 1788 captained by Arthur Philip and are also made of convicts, soldiers and their families. Now, the migration pattern has changed and migrants are coming from all over the world. To better understand migration patterns and what the immigrants are doing in Australia, this visualisation will explore, jobs, migration steams, age of migrants and which are the popular states to migrate to.

Origins of Migrants

Above is a choropleth map depicting Australia’s overseas-born population. There are a total of 8,576,680 people that had migrated and became a citizen of Australia in 2024. Of these countries, China, India, Vietnam, USA, and the United Kingdom has the most amount of people that migrated to Australia. Moreover, Australia has immigrants from all over the world, across all continent. This signifies that Australia is a popular and sought after countries to live in.

Migration Stream

Most new arrivals in 2023–24 entered via the Skill Stream with 72.2%. Then, family stream is second with 27.7% and the least common stream is the special eligibility stream with 0.01%. This means most people that had migrated to Australia are for profession reasons and over a quarter migrated here because of family.

Top Occupations

Registered nurses, accountants, software developers, and engineers dominate. This strong demand in healthcare, technology, and infrastructure sectors illustrates Australia's on going need of these professions as there are not enough locals who are doing these jobs.

Top Languages Spoken

Mandarin, Arabic, Vietnamese, Cantonese, Punjabi, Italian, and Greek are among the top languages that were spoken at home other than English. This means over 7% of people in Australia are speaking one of these 7 languages and not to account hundreds of other languages as well, making Australia a very diversed country to live in.

Net migrants by State 2004-2023

The area chart illustrates net migration trends across Australian states and territories from 2004 to 2023. It highlights how migration fluctuated over time, with steady inflows until a dramatic decline around 2020, marked by the COVID-19 border closures. This sharp dip reflects international travel restrictions and slowed population movement, followed by a strong rebound in 2022–2023 as borders reopened. States such as New South Wales, Victoria, and Queensland consistently attracted the highest number of migrants.

Migrant Age Group Distribution

The bar chart shows the distribution of migrants by age group, revealing that the 20–29 age group makes up the largest proportion of migrants to Australia. This suggests that migration is driven primarily by young adults, likely for education and employment opportunities. The number of migrants gradually declines with age, reflecting lower mobility among older age groups.
